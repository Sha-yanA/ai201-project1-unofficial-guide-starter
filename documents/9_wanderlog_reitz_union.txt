About
The Reitz Union at the University of Florida is a popular spot for students and their friends to enjoy a variety of activities. On Friday nights, visitors can partake in free events such as movie screenings, crafting sessions, and themed food experiences. The outdoor area with umbrellas near the pond provides a pleasant setting for relaxation. Inside, there are numerous dining options to choose from, including favorites like Mi Apa and Panda Express.

Why you should go

1 Enjoy a variety of dining options including Panda Express, Starbucks, Mi Apa, and more
2 Take advantage of free student printing services in the computer lab
3 Explore retail stores for UF swag and books at the bookstore


Know before you go
Visit during off-peak hours to avoid crowds especially around meal times
Utilize student discounts available at various outlets within the union
Check out events happening at Reitz Union for additional entertainment opportunities
If driving, familiarize yourself with parking garage layout beforehand to avoid confusion

Mentioned on articles
Major on-campus destinations like Ben Hill Griffin Stadium, the O'Connell Center (for performances, sports, conventions, etc.) and the Constans Theater next to the Reitz Union (UF's student union) are a short walk away. You'll also love being central to UF's Student Recreation and Fitness Center, the 2nd largest student fitness center on campus. Membership is included with UF enrollment and students get in with their Gator ID, so be sure to make use of this great facility!

From popular food options like Panda Express and Starbucks to services such as free student printing, career resources, and even STI testing at Gator Care, this place has it all. The outdoor seating by the pond provides a serene study spot while inside you can find everything from retail stores selling UF merchandise to entertainment options like bowling alleys and movie theaters. Visitors praise the cleanliness, helpful staff, and diverse offerings that make the Reitz Union a must-visit destination on campus.
